Frequently Asked questions

The requirements for renting a car may vary depending on the car rental company and the country you're in. In general, you will need a valid driver's license, a credit card in your name, and be over a certain age (usually 21 or 25). Some rental companies may also require additional documents such as proof of insurance or a passport.

Most car rental companies offer a range of insurance options, including collision damage waiver, personal accident insurance, and theft protection. Collision damage waiver covers damage to the rental car in the event of an accident, while personal accident insurance provides coverage for medical expenses in case of injury. Theft protection covers the cost of the car if it's stolen. It's important to review the insurance options carefully and ask any questions before selecting coverage to ensure you're adequately protected.

Many car rental companies require renters to be at least 21 years old, and some may have additional age restrictions. If you're under 25, you may be subject to a young driver surcharge, which can vary depending on the company and location. Some rental companies may also require additional documentation or restrictions, such as limiting the types of cars available for rental. Be sure to check with the car rental company beforehand to understand their policies and fees for renting as a young driver.
